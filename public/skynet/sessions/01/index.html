02  The big idea

SKYNET doesn’t end at the wall anymore.

The old model: SKYNET takes the brief, produces insights, hands them to the client. Most of it dies. Insights pile up. Clients don’t act. The work goes nowhere.

The new model: SKYNET takes the brief, produces insights, produces the creative, ships it. The wall gets bypassed. The work — and the money — moves.

That’s the eight weeks. That’s the through-line.

03  The framework

The Handshake.

Every AI creative workflow runs on a 10–80–10 split.

10%
Human
Brief, context, references, direction. The setup.
80%
AI
Generation, iteration, production. The volume.
10%
Human
Editing, checking, finishing, brand alignment. The polish.

Without the human bookends, you get AI slop. With them, you get work that ships.

The trap: 80% of the value lives in the 20% human work. The middle scales. The ends are where craft happens.
04  What to remember

What to remember.

This is not Thursday morning training.
You’re building a category. Not learning a tool.
Rise together.
Share workflows, outputs, references. Teams that share win. Teams that hoard lose.

Claude as the brain. Flora as the canvas. The production models I’ll tell you when to use.

05  Helpful hints

Helpful hints.

Thirty minutes a day on AI.
Not work. Read, watch, scroll. Get it in your feeds. Live it.
SISO — Shit in, Shit Out.
This goes for your prompts, context and well, almost everything we do.
Your “key eye” matters more than your prompts.
Aesthetic taste is the thing AI can’t generate. That’s what we’re building.
Share workflows in Flora.
If it works for you, it should work for the team. Repeatable processes scale.
