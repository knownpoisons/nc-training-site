04  /  Internal — team only

The field guide.

What they'll say, and what'll go wrong. Both are predictable — which is the good news.

Keep this open
Nothing here is theory. Every objection below gets asked, and every bump below gets hit — by everyone, usually in the first month. Having the answer ready is the difference between holding the line and folding on the spot.
01  Objections

What they'll say.

Loaded answers. Don't improvise these in the moment — the improvised version is always the one that gives something away.

Shouldn't it be cheaper, then?
→
Everywhere else, more and faster costs more. Three pairs of shoes in two days versus one pair in a week — you pay more. Happy to charge more if that's the direction. Or: same budget, ten times the output.
Can we have the files for our socials?
→
That's a different service and a different price. Not a no — a separate conversation. This is production for the media we run for you. Book the other one in properly.
Is this AI? We have a policy on that.
→
It's a creative service. Humans at both ends — brief and direction in, brand check and craft out. The tools in the middle are just fast. Don't get drawn into a tooling debate — you're selling the outcome.
It looks AI.
→
Then it isn't finished. That's the 10% out, and it's on us — the bad ones never leave the building. Never argue this one. They're right.
Can we jump on a call and go through it together?
→
Written or a Loom. A call is how we lose half of what you said. Everything in writing goes straight into the work and nothing gets misremembered.
We need it tomorrow.
→
That's a queue jump, not a bigger job. You're not charging for more work — you're charging because someone else moved down the pipeline.
Our brand guidelines are really strict.
→
Good — guidelines are context, and context is exactly what makes this work. Send them. Strict brands get better results, not worse.
Why are we paying the same if it takes you less time?
→
You're not buying hours. You're buying speed to market and the volume that comes with it. Cost per deliverable is a dead way to price this.
02  Bumps

What'll go wrong first.

Everyone hits these. Knowing they're coming is most of the fix.

  1. Your first outputs will disappoint you.
    The problem is the brief. Always. Not the model. Change the input, not the tool.
  2. You'll take the live call "just this once."
    The process dies right there. Once is the whole thing. Hold it the first time and you never have to hold it again.
  3. You'll accept a half-brief to be helpful.
    Then you wait, then you guess, then you blame the tool. No brief, no start.
  4. You'll show them twenty.
    Show three. Play with fifty in your own canvas. Paradox of choice is real and it will cost you the round.
  5. You'll undercharge the first one.
    Set your floor before the conversation, not during it. You never know if you don't ask.
  6. Product integrity will drift when you regenerate.
    That's exactly why one round matters. Every regeneration risks the thing you already got right.
  7. You'll burn the good model early.
    Cheap model to lock the prompt. Then upgrade for the final render. Don't spend the good credits learning.
  8. You'll go quiet mid-round.
    Silence gets filled with client anxiety — and anxious clients ask for calls. A one-line "on track, Thursday" costs nothing.
  9. Someone will ask for the files.
    Under a compliment, on the spot, you'll say yes. Have the answer ready before it happens.
  10. One will come back bad and they'll be annoyed.
    You're their agency — you're in the meeting next Tuesday regardless. Own it fast, fix it, don't get defensive. The variance was disclosed; the recovery is the relationship.
The one that matters most
Every one of these is a first-month problem, not a forever problem. Hold the line early and they stop happening.
