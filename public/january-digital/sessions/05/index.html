02  The framework

The product image workflow.

Four nodes on the canvas, in order. The LLM node doesn't make the picture — it writes the instructions that make the picture. That's the part people skip.

1
Source / Reference
Carries the world: mood, lighting, style, energy you want.
2
Product Image
The thing the ad sells. Logo visible, exact SKU.
3
LLM (Claude)
Reads both images and writes a system prompt for the image model.
4
Image Node
Renders the result. Run a few — two to four, not twenty.
"If it's a maybe, it's a no." And when it goes from maybe to a no — delete it. What is really good about AI is you can make it again 50 times over. Assets, creative, photography, videos have become fully disposable.— Jeremy Somers
03  The insight

Connect, or don't.

Whether you wire the reference image into the node or not changes everything:

Disconnected
The image model leans on its own training knowledge of the brand. It already knows what Carhartt looks like and how to lay a tee on a pile of logs. Surprisingly capable — but it won't replicate your exact reference.
Connected
The output stays close to the source composition. Use this when you need to land near a specific look. Get the replica, then nudge — "swap the logs for tropical leaves" — so it becomes a remix, not a copy.
"Everything we do and everything we're going to do in creativity is just remixing something else. We are doing this for money — we are not doing this for art."— Jeremy Somers
04  The strategic edge

You have something no creative shop has.

You are a media agency, not a creative studio — and that means you have hard data on what actually worked. Which format, which environment, which placement drove the click-throughs. That's the bypass from Session 01 in motion.

The move
Pull the client's historical ad performance data and drop it into your Claude project as core context.
Don't read it and decipher it yourself — hand it to the machine, tell it what to look for, and let it surface the directions most likely to perform. Feed results back into the next round of creative.
"You have this third part which nobody else has, which I do not have: you have hard data on what actually works. That's where you have influence over the client and what you make."— Jeremy Somers

06  What to remember

Keep these close.

If it's a maybe, it's a no.
The instant a keeper slides to "maybe," delete it. Don't sit with it. Regenerate.
Assets are disposable.
The keep-everything, V2_final_final era is over. Make it again — it's free-ish and fast.
Run a few, not twenty.
Two to four variants per node. Twenty at once scrambles your brain because they're all good now.
The report is everything.
You're a media agency. Run all of it through the lens of what the data says actually worked.
Name your workflows.
Shared workspace = a wall of "untitled." Title everything, drag into your personal folder, delete what you don't need.
Fauna is a how-to, not a chatbot.
The in-platform agent knows Flora back to front — ask it how to connect a node, make an animation, anything.
