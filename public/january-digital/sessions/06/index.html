01  What we covered

One flat lay, infinite concepts.

Everything in Flora — no matter how tangled the canvas looks — is one of three nodes: text, image, video. Once the chain is built, a whole new concept costs one or two changed words. Ten concepts in twelve minutes. But the economics only hold if the feedback loop stays as fast as the production loop.

03  Scaling

One image becomes twenty.

Select the chain, ⌘D to duplicate, change one or two words — new world.

Present ~5, not 20
Paradox of choice: twenty options gets you no decision. Five gets a winner.
90% finished at concept
Clients don't have to imagine anymore — they react. Nitpicking means they've accepted.
Duplicate, don't rebuild
⌘D duplicates the whole chain. Change the scene word. New concept in seconds.
Download into a folder
Good enough for concept stage is the bar. Originals go in a round-one folder — not Downloads.
"Clients don't have an imagination. My entire career we've had to get some sort of imagination out of them. Now we're at the point where we're giving them close to finished."— Jeremy Somers
04  The protocol

The client feedback protocol.

This is the part that keeps the margin.

Non-negotiable
Async only. No live creative reviews.
Live calls produce emotional, vague, unrecorded feedback. Async is documented, non-debatable, and feeds straight into Claude for the changes.
Loom it
The free 5-minute cap forces succinct feedback. Recorded, timestamped, feeds straight into the next iteration.
Two rounds, defined upfront
Round 1: anything goes — concept swaps, new worlds. Round 2: refinements only. Agreed before any work starts.
Send the one-sheet
What acceptable feedback looks like, delivered with every round of assets.
"You are going to regenerate the entire image. You're gonna lose all the hours of work on product integrity and lighting and brand positioning — all of it."— Luisa Melo (the can-rim shadow)

06  What to remember

Keep these close.

Bad output is on us, not the model.
Diagnose the prompt, change the word, run again.
Set your default model.
Project settings → preferences → default model. Don't let a new image node fall back to Flux.
Inputs left, outputs right.
Drag a node's plus into empty canvas and Flora tells you what it can become.
Client services is becoming creative services.
Different relationship, real boundaries, real financial consequences.
Use render time.
While a node generates you can't edit its prompt — so edit the next concept, queue another run.
The pitch writes itself.
A crew, a two-day shoot, $200K and three months — or ads in-market in weeks.
