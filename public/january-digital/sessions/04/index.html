04  The canvas

Flōra — four nodes, that's it.

Flōra is an infinite canvas (think Figma) that calls ~80 image/video/text models through one interface. No separate ChatGPT account, no separate anything. Connect nodes with +, run with the arrow (set to 5 options per click), and duplicate with Cmd-D.

U
Upload
Drag a product image onto the canvas.
T
Text / LLM
Chat with Claude, GPT, or Gemini for prompting.
I
Image
Generates images. Paste your Claude output into the prompt box.
V
Video
Coming in a future session.

The honest truth: most of your work is one image node with a pasted prompt. The big canvas of wired-up nodes is for your eye, not a requirement.

05  Which models to use

Two models. Pin them both.

Default
Nano Banana 2 (Google)
The current best. Set as your default in admin. Jeremy's pick when there are people in the shot. Lineage: Nano Banana → Nano Banana Pro → Nano Banana 2.
For product
GPT Image 2 (OpenAI)
Better for product consistency. No separate ChatGPT account needed — all billed through Flōra.

Workflow: run both at low res / 1K (faster, cheaper), pick the winner, then regenerate the keeper at 2K for the finish. Both models pull real-world context natively — they know what a "vintage Porsche" or a brand looks like without you spelling it out.

★  From the team

Luisa's three moves for creative prompting.

From the team's first live production case — the tips that actually work:

1. Put Claude in the CD's seat
Most of us were never trained to describe an aesthetic. Telling Claude to think like a creative director covers the gap you can't articulate.
2. Give it an anchor
A reference image, or a described aesthetic. Without an anchor it has nothing to aim taste at.
3. Iterate as many times as you need
Anchors plus reps. That's the whole game.
06  What to remember

Keep these close.

The problem is always me.
Bad output is an underbriefed teammate, not a broken machine. Find the missing letter — it's almost always context.
Context is the one everyone skips.
The origin of the idea is context. The brand history is context. Hand it over.
Don't hoard files — regenerate.
The era of hoodie_flat_lay_V2_final_final.psd is over. Keep a clean workspace; delete what you don't need.
One node does almost everything.
Resist the urge to learn all of Flōra. Image node, paste the prompt, run. Do it 25 times and you've got a campaign.
Test cheap, finish expensive.
Always start at low / 1K. Only go 2K on the one you love.
Feedback is data.
Accept client feedback async only — written, or a Loom. Screenshot the feedback deck and feed it straight into the image node.
07  Homework

Do this before Session 5.

Flōra access lands Monday June 22. This is a play-week, not a produce-week. You're not waiting for a live brief to open the tool — open it to mess around, break things, and build the muscle so the tool is invisible by the time real work shows up.

First · Activate (Monday)
When the invite hits your inbox, activate your account. Pin Nano Banana 2 (default) and GPT Image 2 so they're top of the list.
Watch one or two Flōra tutorials from the playlist (link in Notion). ~3 minutes each — watch at 1.25×.
1 · Replicate a saved reference
Pull one piece you love from your Instagram saves. Drop an image node, paste a prompt, and try to recreate that reference in Flōra.
Run 5 options. Don't expect it to nail it first try — that's the point.
2 · Make 10–15 variations
Take whatever you started and push it. Change one thing at a time. Duplicate the node (Cmd-D) and run Nano Banana 2 vs GPT Image 2 on the same prompt.
Volume is the lesson. You're after 10–15 variations, not one perfect image.
3 · Try one video (optional but encouraged)
Drop a video node and make something move. We cover video properly next week — this is just to see it work.
If you've got time over the long weekend.
