02  The offer

What you sell now.

One page. Fill the brackets, make it theirs, send it. Sells the shift, the edge, and the value — never the tool.

"We make it"
You don't just plan where creative runs. You produce it — stills and motion, on brand, ready to run in the media you manage.
The data edge
A creative shop makes something beautiful. You make something that works, because you can see the scoreboard. Same team, same numbers, same channels.
10 / 80 / 10
Human in (brief, taste, direction), production at machine speed, human out (on brand, pixel perfect). The speed comes from the middle. The quality comes from the two ends.
Not per asset, not per hour
You're selling the creative system and the speed to market. Pricing is a conversation, not a number on the page.
"Double the content in half the time" undersells it. In practice it's closer to ten times the content in a tenth of the time — it just didn't have the same ring to it.— Jeremy Somers
03  The rules

How a round works.

Send this before the work starts. Every rule pre-agreed, while everyone's still excited.

1
Complete Brief
Nothing starts without the brief, copy, and assets confirmed and final.
2
Round One
Go wide. Change anything: concept, background, world. This is the window.
3
Creative Control
What you specify, we build exactly. What you don't, we decide.
4
Then It's Locked
After round one: genuine errors only. This rule keeps it fast.
5
Async Feedback
Written or Loom. No live calls. Nothing lost, nothing debatable.
6
Expect Variance
5–15% natural variance on regeneration. The trade for the speed.
7
Approval Chain
Named upfront. No surprise stakeholders mid-round.
The trade, in one line
Give us creative control over what you didn't specify, and you get speed, volume, and creative shaped by what actually performs.
04  Selling it in

The internal playbook.

How you bring an account you already run into the new process. Not a cold pitch — a warm start.

Stop saying AI
This is a creative service. The tools happen to be fast. Nobody ever asked if you used Photoshop.
Proof first, pitch second
Improve the creative on an account you already manage, watch what it does, then talk with numbers in your hand.
The pre-frame
Tell them upfront: this will feel uncomfortable. They're trading some control for the speed. Say it now; callback later.
Volunteer the limits
Name what it can't do before anyone asks. The biggest trust-builder you have.
The boundary
You make the ads you run. Anything outside the media plan is a different service with a different price.
The endgame
Creative production bundled as a percentage of the media spend. The move only JD can make.
"Proof first. Pitch second. Nobody else in this market can do that — a freelancer needs permission before they can show you anything. You just need a campaign you're already running."— Jeremy Somers
05  The field guide

What they'll say, and what'll go wrong.

Both are predictable. That's the good news.

Objections
"Shouldn't it be cheaper?"
More and faster costs more everywhere else.
"Can we have the files?"
Different service, different price.
"It looks AI."
Then it isn't finished. Never argue this one.
"Can we jump on a call?"
Written or Loom. A call is how you lose half of what they said.
First-month bumps
Your first outputs will disappoint you
The brief is the problem. Always.
You'll take the live call "just this once"
Once is the whole thing.
You'll show them twenty
Show three. Paradox of choice costs you the round.
Someone will ask for the files
Under a compliment. Have the answer ready.
The one that matters most
Every one of these is a first-month problem, not a forever problem. Hold the line early and they stop happening.
