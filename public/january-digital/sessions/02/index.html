★  Remember this

The three questions.

Ask these of every piece of work you see. Every time. They're the difference between scrolling past something and actually reading it.

1. What is it?
The literal thing. A poster, a film, a campaign, a product shot. Name the format before you judge the work.
2. What's the idea?
Not the execution — the concept underneath. What is the piece actually saying? What's it trying to make you feel or do?
3. What's the unexpected thing?
The part that surprises you. The choice that didn't have to be there but makes the whole thing work. That's the creative decision.
Most people stop at "I like it" or "I don't." These three questions force you past taste and into craft. That's where creative direction lives.

Do this before Session 3.

Read this — we didn't get to it live. We ran out of clock before the homework, so here's the full version. It's the work that makes Session 3 land.

1 · Practise the three questions
On every piece of work you see this week, run them: What is it? What's the idea? What's the unexpected thing?
Just notice. By Session 4 it should be automatic.
2 · Share one piece in Slack
Find something you admire creatively. Drop the link in #project-creative-training with a quick explanation.
Say what it is, why you're sharing it, what you like about the creativity — and answer the three questions: What is it? What's the idea? What's the unexpected thing?
