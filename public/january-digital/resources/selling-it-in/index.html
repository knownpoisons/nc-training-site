03  /  Internal — team only

Selling it in.

How you bring an account you already run into the new production process.

Internal — do not send to clients
You're not cold-selling strangers. These are people who already trust you — that's an advantage most of this game never gets. The job isn't convincing them you're capable. It's setting the process up right so the speed survives contact with a real client.
01  First, the internal sell

It dies inside, or not at all.

Before the client hears a word, someone at JD has to say yes — an account lead, and anyone whose job feels adjacent to this. That's where it dies, if it dies. Not at the client.

So don't open with a pitch. Open with proof, and make it small: you don't need permission to make the ads you already run better. You control that media. Improve the creative on an account you already manage, watch what it does, then have the conversation with numbers in your hand.

The play
Proof first. Pitch second.

Nobody else in this market can do that — a freelancer needs permission before they can show you anything. You just need a campaign you're already running.

02  The reframes

Pick the one the moment needs.

Rule one — stop saying AI
Aim never to say it. This is a creative service — creative people doing creative things — and the tools happen to be fast. Sell speed, output and money, not technology. Nobody ever asked if you used Photoshop.
"Shouldn't it be cheaper, then?"
In every other part of life, more and faster costs more. Order three pairs of shoes in two days instead of one pair in a week — you pay more. So: happy to charge more if that's the direction. Or keep the budget where it is and massively increase the output and cut the time.
"Clients don't have an imagination"
Your whole career you've had to drag a vision out of people because you couldn't show them final work. Now you hand them something near-finished — so all that's left is nitpicking. And nitpicking means they've already accepted the concept. Walk in with it half-done and it's half-won.
Ads that make money
Set the altitude honestly. This is for work that performs and gets to market fast — not a print for the wall to be scrutinised at Cannes. A lot of creative is only a little bit creative, and that's fine. The point is speed back to market so it can earn.
03  The pre-frame

Say it while everyone's happy.

This is the single move that makes every rule hold later. Do it at kickoff, every time.

Script · the discomfort pre-frame
This new process is going to feel uncomfortable at some point. You'll be wiggling in your seat because you don't have the full control you're used to. That's the trade — you're relinquishing some of that control in exchange for the speed and the output. I'm telling you now, so when we get to that moment, you'll remember we agreed to it.

Then when it arrives — "we also just thought of…" mid-round — you get to say: this is the uncomfortable conversation I told you we might have. Here it is. A callback lands. A surprise starts a fight.

04  The boundary

You make the ads you run.

This is the line that protects the whole business, and you have to hold it. The creative you produce is for the media you manage. It is not a free creative studio for the client's owned social, their brand campaigns, or anything outside the media plan.

The moment the work is good, someone will ask for the files. Have the answer ready before it happens — because on the spot, under a compliment, you'll say yes.

Script · when they ask for the files
Really glad you like it. That's a different service though — what we're doing here is production for the media we run for you, and it's priced against that. If you want assets for your owned channels or a broader brand campaign, that's a separate scope and I'd love to talk about it properly. Let's book that in.

It's not a no. It's a different conversation, with a different price. That distinction is the entire point.

05  Honesty as a tool

Volunteer the limits.

Name what it can't do before anyone asks. The tool can't blow a bubble-gum bubble or tie a shoelace in macro. Text-heavy packaging across fifty SKUs — tell them to use 3D instead. If a real shoot is the right call, say so.

Volunteering what it can't do is the biggest trust-builder you have. Don't oversell the machine.

06  The endgame

The move only you can make.

No freelancer and no creative shop can do this, because they don't run the media. You do.

The shape
A chunk for the creative work per campaign — plus a percentage of the spend on top.

And when you can prove what you do works: sell a percentage of the wins, over and above your normal rates. When something's firing, you can make new versions and get them back in front of people in under a day, while it's still working. That's where the whole value lives.

07  Quick reference

Say this. Not that.

Say
  • Faster, more output, on brand
  • Drives performance
  • We already know what's working for you
  • Speed to market
  • That's a different service — let's talk
Don't say
  • "AI"
  • "Cheaper"
  • "Unlimited revisions"
  • "We'll keep going till you're happy"
  • Anything that opens the door to endless rounds
