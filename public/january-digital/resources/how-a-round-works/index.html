How a round works.

The document you send before the work starts. Every rule pre-agreed, while everyone's still excited.

How to use this
Send it at kickoff, not when something's already going wrong. Agreeing to it up front is what turns the uncomfortable moment later into a callback rather than a fight. [BRAND] Creative Production — how a round works

To move at this speed, the process has a shape.

Here it is up front, so there are no surprises later. Agreeing to this is how you get the volume and the turnaround.

1

A complete brief before we start

Nothing gets made until the brief, the copy and the assets are confirmed and final. No half-briefs, no "we'll figure out the headline later." The whole model only works — and only stays fast — when the machine isn't waiting on missing pieces. Our brief template takes about an hour.

2

Round one is yours — go wide

In the first round, change anything. The concept, the background, the cast, the whole world. Be as prescriptive and detailed as you like — that level of specificity is welcome. This is your window. Use it.

3

The creative-control trade

Whatever you specify, we build exactly. Whatever you don't specify, we and the system decide — and then that's decided. If we deliver what you asked for and it's right, we're not pixel-pushing it afterward.

If something's genuinely wrong, of course we fix it. That's not what this means.

4

Then it's locked

After round one, changes are limited to genuine errors: the product's warped, a logo's misspelt. That's it. This one rule is what keeps the whole thing fast — and fast is the entire point.

5

Feedback is asynchronous

Written notes, or a five-minute Loom — not a live call. Two reasons: written feedback goes straight into the system so nothing gets lost or misremembered, and it's on the record, so it isn't up for debate later. Succinct beats a meeting.

6

Expect a little variance

Every time the work regenerates there's a small natural variance we don't fully control. It's the nature of the tool, not a mistake. You're trading a sliver of that wildness for enormous gains in output, speed and cost — knowingly.

7

Everyone who approves is named up front

Anyone who needs to sign off — a superior, a brand lead, legal — is on the kickoff, or their feedback doesn't land mid-round. No surprise stakeholder rewriting the work after we're locked. We agree the chain now.

8

What this covers

This is creative production for the media we run for you — the ads, in the channels we manage. Work for your owned social, brand campaigns or anything outside the media plan is a different service with a different scope and price. Happy to have that conversation separately.

The trade, in one line
Give us creative control over what you didn't specify, and you get speed, volume, and creative shaped by what actually performs.
