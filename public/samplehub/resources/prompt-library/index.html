Claude Prompt
Library.

12 starter prompts for media and creative operators learning to prompt Claude well.

These are reference examples — not scripts to paste daily. Dip in when starting a new conversation or project, internalise the pattern, then adapt for your own work. Every template has [BRACKETS] where your context goes.

The framework
I · C · C
Instructions · Context · Constraints
I
Instructions
Define the task and the action you want taken.
C
Context
Your role, objectives, background, anything relevant to the task.
C
Constraints
Rules, style, tone, length, output format.

Every good prompt has three parts. Most weak prompts are just missing one of them. Instructions tell Claude what to do. Context gives it what it needs to do it well. Constraints shape how the answer comes back. The twelve prompts below all use this structure — read them as examples of the pattern, not scripts.

01  Research

Ask better questions.

01
Tell Me What I Don't Know
Poke holes in a half-formed idea, brief, or strategy. Find what you're missing, overweighting, or don't understand yet.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Tell me everything important that I do not know, am underestimating, or am misunderstanding about this. Find the blindspot.

CONTEXT
Here's the idea, brief, or strategy and what I currently think about it:
[Paste your thinking here]

CONSTRAINTS
Be direct. Prioritise what would actually change my decision over what's merely interesting.
Example use cases
  • You've drafted a creative brief and want to stress-test your assumptions before you present it
  • You've got a campaign idea and want to know what you're not seeing about the audience or the insight
  • You're working through a production workflow and want to find the gaps in your logic
02
God Mode Research
Understand a topic from every angle. Theory, expert perspectives, history, real-world tradeoffs. Not surface level.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Give me a deep, working understanding of [TOPIC] from every angle.

CONTEXT
Why I need it and what I'll use it for:
[Paste here]

CONSTRAINTS
Cover: theoretical foundations, expert perspectives and case studies, historical patterns, real-world tradeoffs, common misconceptions, emerging or counterintuitive shifts. Give me a working understanding, not definitions.
Example use cases
  • You're building a campaign around a trend and need to know it inside-out before the brief
  • You're pitching a strategic idea and need the research behind it
  • You're evaluating a new platform or format and need to understand how it actually works
03
10X Learning Speed
Master a skill or tool fast. Core concepts, mental models, common mistakes, and a path to competence.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Build me the fastest path to competence in [SKILL/TOOL].

CONTEXT
Where I'm starting from and why I need this:
[Paste here]

CONSTRAINTS
Break it into: core concepts first, the mental models that unlock the rest, the most common mistakes, the 20 percent that gets me 80 percent of the way, quick wins for today, and next-level moves once I've got the basics.
Example use cases
  • You need to learn a production tool before your next project
  • You're onboarding a new team member and want to speed up their ramp
  • You're deciding whether to build in-house or outsource and need to understand the skill gap
04
Second Brain
Turn a pile of notes, research, or ideas into a structure you can actually use.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Organise the information below into something I can reference and act on.

CONTEXT
[Paste your notes, research, ideas — brain dump it]

CONSTRAINTS
Structure as: core themes, supporting detail under each, actionable takeaways, open questions or gaps, and how the pieces connect. Make it scannable, not just reorganised.
Example use cases
  • You've collected competitor research and audience insight for a brief and need it organised before you brief the team
  • You've got feedback and direction scattered across emails and docs and need it consolidated
  • You're building a reference set for a campaign and need it structured so others can navigate it
05
Mental Model Expansion
Understand something through multiple frameworks. Each one reveals a different angle.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Give me the most powerful mental models for understanding [TOPIC].

CONTEXT
What I'm actually trying to figure out:
[Paste here]

CONSTRAINTS
For each model: plain-English definition, how it applies to a real decision, where it breaks down, and one concrete example. I want to think differently, not just know more facts.
Example use cases
  • You're trying to understand why a campaign didn't land and want frameworks to diagnose it
  • You're building strategy and want to see the problem through more than one lens
  • You're evaluating creative work and want language for why something works, not just whether it does
02  Writing

Say it sharper.

06
Message Polisher
Tighten something that isn't landing. Clearer, sharper, same voice.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Rewrite the text below as the sharpest version of what I'm trying to say.

CONTEXT
What I wrote, and who it's for:
[Paste your message, email, brief, or copy]

CONSTRAINTS
Clearer, more persuasive, better paced. Keep my voice and personality — don't make it sound like a robot wrote it.
Example use cases
  • You've drafted a client email and it feels off, tighten it before you send
  • You've written a brief that's too long or unclear, compress it without losing meaning
  • You've got social or ad copy that isn't punchy enough
07
Perfect Email
Make an email land. No misreads, no extra reads, crisp and clear.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Write me an email about [SUBJECT] that's impossible to misunderstand.

CONTEXT
The point I'm making, and what they need to know first:
[Paste here]

CONSTRAINTS
Tone: [professional / collaborative / direct / warm — or describe it]. Keep it tight. No filler, no burying the ask.
Example use cases
  • You're sending a brief to the production team and need zero confusion about the ask
  • You're following up on feedback and need to be clear on next steps without sounding defensive
  • You're communicating a decision or change and need it to land without ambiguity
08
Ultimate Writing Improvement
A full pass on a piece of writing. Tighter sentences, better flow, stronger rhythm.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Do a full improvement pass on the writing below.

CONTEXT
The piece, and what it's for:
[Paste the full thing]

CONSTRAINTS
Tighter sentences, clearer logic, stronger transitions, sharper rhythm, vivid language where it earns it. Keep my meaning and voice intact.
Example use cases
  • You've written a brief or proposal and want a final polish before it ships
  • You've got a pitch script you want tighter and more compelling
  • You're writing a case study and want it readable and persuasive
03  Productivity & Learning

Think clearer, move faster.

09
Skill Accelerator
Learn something in a compressed timeframe. Structured plan, daily practice, real progress.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Build me a plan to get proficient at [SKILL] in [TIMEFRAME].

CONTEXT
Why I need it, what "proficient" looks like to me, and my real constraints (time per day, tools I have):
[Paste here]

CONSTRAINTS
Include daily drills, real projects to test myself on, early quick wins for momentum, weekly check-in questions, and next-level moves. Doable, not theoretical.
Example use cases
  • You've got three weeks to learn a new production tool before a launch
  • You're ramping a junior team member in a specific area
  • You want to pick up a craft skill that keeps coming up in the work
10
Fix My Thinking
Find the flawed assumptions, biases, and gaps in how you're reasoning about something.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Analyse my reasoning below and find the flaws.

CONTEXT
How I'm currently thinking about [SITUATION/DECISION]:
[Paste your reasoning]

CONSTRAINTS
Identify flawed assumptions, biases that might be distorting it, logic that's missing or doesn't hold, and a clearer way to think about it. Be direct — I'd rather know now.
Example use cases
  • You're making a call on a campaign direction and want your reasoning checked before you commit
  • You're stuck on a problem and suspect you're framing it wrong
  • You're weighing a decision and want the blindspots surfaced before the meeting
04  Foundations

The habits underneath.

These two are the base habits ICC sits on top of: how to hand Claude your actual material, and how to work with it across rounds instead of one-shotting it.

11
Load Your Material
The habit that makes everything else work. Give Claude the material before you ask for anything.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Read everything I'm giving you before you do anything. If something's unclear or missing, ask me before you start.

CONTEXT
What I'm working on, plus the actual material:
[Paste or upload your documents, brief, references, notes, examples]

CONSTRAINTS
What good looks like to me — tone, format, anything non-negotiable:
[Paste here]
Example use cases
  • You're starting a new project and want to load the brand guide, brief, and references up front
  • You've got a brand document and a reference you saw elsewhere and want both in play
  • You want Claude working from your actual material, not its best guess
12
Iteration / Feedback
Prompting isn't one shot. Redirect the output instead of starting over.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
Revise your last output using the feedback below. Show me one version, then we'll refine from there.

CONTEXT
What's working and what isn't:
Working: [what to keep]
Not working: [what's off, and why]

CONSTRAINTS
The specific change I want this round: [tighter / different angle / new tone / etc.]. One version, not five.
Example use cases
  • Claude's first draft is close but not right, and you want to steer rather than restart
  • You want to explore a different angle without losing what already worked
  • You're building something across a few rounds and want each pass to get sharper
05  Flora

System prompts for the canvas.

Copy-paste these into Flora's text nodes. They turn a flat product image into a scene concept and a finished still into motion — ICC, written tight, aimed at the image and video models.

13
Image · Flat Lay to Scene Concept
Turn a single flat-lay product image into a photographic scene concept for a Meta ad.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
You are an executive creative director. You'll be given a single flat-lay product image. Write one image prompt that places this exact product into a scene.

CONTEXT
This is [product / brand] for a Meta ad. The look is photographic and realistic — on-brand, never stylised or illustrated.

CONSTRAINTS
Keep the product's exact colour, material, shape and proportions. Name one concrete environment with real detail. State the camera angle and the lighting direction. Write it detailed and highly tactile — real surfaces, textures and materials you can almost feel, photographic realism. Output only the prompt: raw prompt text, one paragraph, nothing else.
Example use cases
  • You have a product flat lay and need a scene concept for a Meta campaign
  • You want the image model to place the product in a realistic environment without losing brand fidelity
  • You're generating multiple concepts by duplicating the chain and changing the environment word
14
Video · Still to Motion (Fill the Brackets)
Turn a finished still into a short-form video prompt — static locked camera, product centred.
 
INSTRUCTIONS
You are an executive creative director. You'll be given a finished still image. Take the movement idea in the brackets and write one video prompt.

MY MOVEMENT IDEA
[ ]

CONTEXT
This is a product shot for a Meta ad — photographic and realistic, on-brand. The product stays in frame, as centred as possible, from the first second to the last.

CONSTRAINTS
Static locked camera — no camera movement, no zoom. Everything stays completely still except the movement above. If a person appears they're implied, never the hero — a shadow, or a hand or arm that enters and leaves. Write it detailed and tactile, photographic realism. Output only the prompt: raw prompt text, one paragraph, nothing else.
Example use cases
  • You've got a keeper still and want to bring it to life for short-form video
  • Your movement idea is simple — "a hand enters and picks it up" — and you need the model to fill in the cinematic detail
  • You're building the full chain: flat lay → scene concept → still → video
